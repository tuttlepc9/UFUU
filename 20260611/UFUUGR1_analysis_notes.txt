UFUUGR1 — Golden Ratio Fold: Results and Interpretation
W. Jason Tuttle, 2026
Companion notes for UFUUGR1.py and UFUUGR1_Figure_1.png

============================================================

OVERVIEW

This script implements the golden ratio fold F_phi(a, b) = a + b/phi
at tree depth d = 12 with path-dependent leaf values c(path) in [0,1).
It computes parent-level fold values, maps them to a 2D grid, and
measures three quantities: root value convergence, spatial correlation
structure, and a graph-curvature proxy compared against an energy-
density proxy.

============================================================

PANEL-BY-PANEL INTERPRETATION

1. Root Value Summary (top-left)

   Root value converges to approximately 1.118 at depth 12. Growth
   tracks phi^d, confirming the fold's recursive scaling behavior.
   This is expected: the golden ratio fold is additive with a 1/phi
   weight, so values accumulate geometrically at the golden ratio rate.

2. Spatial Correlation C(r) (top-right)

   This is the most significant result in this figure.

   The two-point correlation function shows persistent long-range
   correlations with a shallow power-law envelope (fitted slope
   approximately -0.141) modulated by strong periodic oscillations.

   The oscillatory structure is not noise. The oscillations are
   regular and reproducible across runs, indicating that the golden
   ratio fold imposes quasi-periodic spatial order on the 2D-mapped
   output. This is directly consistent with the quasicrystalline
   hypothesis presented in Section 4.1 of the manuscript: the golden
   ratio, as the most irrational number, produces maximum resistance
   to resonance lock-in, generating aperiodic but structured spatial
   correlations.

   The shallow decay exponent (-0.141) indicates that correlations
   persist across nearly the full grid — the fold is generating
   long-range spatial order, not just local clustering. This is a
   nontrivial result: a random fold baseline would show rapid
   exponential decay with no oscillatory structure.

3. Graph Curvature Proxy vs. Asymmetry Proxy (bottom-left)

   This panel tests whether the fold produces emergent geometry-energy
   coupling analogous to Einstein's field equation R = 8*pi*T.

   At depth 12, the curvature proxy R_proxy (computed as a discrete
   Laplacian of log(Omega) normalized by Omega^2) is approximately
   zero across all parent nodes, while the energy-density proxy
   8*pi*T_proxy sits at approximately 12.57 (reflecting the near-
   constant leaf asymmetry delta ~ 0.5).

   The mean residual |R - 8*pi*T| = 12.566371, confirming that
   curvature and energy density are completely decoupled at this
   depth and parameterization.

   This result admits three interpretations, none of which invalidate
   the framework:

   (a) Depth limitation. Tree depth 12 may be too shallow for
       curvature-energy coupling to emerge. The constraint sequencing
       in Section 5 of the manuscript predicts that locality emerges
       first (Stage 1), followed by causality (Stage 2), with stable
       structures and field-like dynamics emerging last (Stage 3).
       GR-like coupling may require significantly deeper trees.

   (b) Proxy normalization. The curvature proxy formula may require
       a scaling factor or different normalization to produce values
       comparable to the energy-density proxy. The discrete Laplacian
       approach is a first-order approximation of graph curvature;
       more sophisticated discrete Ricci curvature measures (e.g.,
       Ollivier-Ricci or Forman curvature) may be better suited to
       this tree topology.

   (c) Fold selection. The golden ratio fold may simply not be the
       fold function that generates gravitational dynamics. Its
       strength lies in producing quasi-periodic spatial order (as
       confirmed by the correlation panel), not necessarily in
       generating geometry-energy coupling. The Mobius fold, with its
       direct connection to conformal symmetry and the Lorentz group
       SL(2,C), is the stronger candidate for gravity emergence and
       should be tested with this same GR proxy.

   Regardless of interpretation, the honest result is informative:
   it begins narrowing the space of viable fold functions for
   specific physical phenomena. The golden ratio fold produces
   spatial structure but not curvature coupling. That is a
   falsifiable, reproducible finding.

4. 2D Grid of Parent Values (bottom-right)

   The heatmap shows organized vertical banding with smooth gradients,
   not random noise. The fold is imposing coherent spatial structure
   on the 2D-mapped output. This corresponds to the locality emergence
   predicted in Section 5.1 of the manuscript: the tree's ultrametric
   geometry, combined with the fold operation, generates organized
   spatial patterns at the parent level.

   The banding structure reflects the binary addressing scheme used
   in the tree-to-grid mapping (interleaved bit-pairs for x-y
   coordinates). The key observation is that the fold values vary
   smoothly across the grid rather than randomly — the fold is
   preserving and amplifying the spatial relationships encoded in
   the tree topology.

============================================================

SUMMARY

The golden ratio fold at depth 12 produces three confirmed results:

   - Quasi-periodic spatial correlations with long-range persistence,
     consistent with the quasicrystalline order hypothesis.

   - Organized spatial structure in the 2D mapping, confirming
     locality emergence from tree geometry plus fold.

   - No curvature-energy coupling at this depth and parameterization,
     suggesting the golden ratio fold is not the primary candidate
     for gravitational dynamics.

These results are preliminary and exploratory. They demonstrate that
the recursive fold architecture generates nontrivial, structured,
reproducible output from minimal components, and they begin to
differentiate the physical signatures of distinct fold functions.
