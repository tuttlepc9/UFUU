============================================================

WHAT THIS TEST DOES

Runs the pure Möbius fold F_m(a, b) = |(phi*z + 1) / (z + phi)|
across multiple tree depths (d = 12, 14, 15, 16, 18, 20, 22, 24)
and measures four quantities at each depth:

   1. Root value convergence
   2. Ultrametric LCA correlation exponent (the paper's natural metric)
   3. Entropy profile (leaf → root) — P4 test
   4. Peak LCA depth of the correlation spike + ratio to total depth

============================================================

SESSION 1 — UNSEEDED RUNS (ufuumob2_loop.py)

Problem identified: compute_ultrametric_correlation() uses
np.random.randint() with no fixed seed. Two consecutive runs on
the same machine produced completely different spike locations
and ultrametric slopes, making the ratio analysis meaningless.

Example of instability across two local runs:

Run 1:
   d=15: peak LCA = 5,  ratio = 0.333, ultra slope = -0.2872
   d=16: peak LCA = 8,  ratio = 0.500, ultra slope = -0.1670
   d=20: peak LCA = 16, ratio = 0.800, ultra slope =  0.1003

Run 2:
   d=15: peak LCA = 9,  ratio = 0.600, ultra slope = -0.9149
   d=16: peak LCA = 12, ratio = 0.750, ultra slope = -0.0739
   d=20: peak LCA = 16, ratio = 0.800, ultra slope = -0.3187

The ultrametric slope and peak LCA location are completely
unstable without a fixed seed. These results cannot be used
for analysis or publication.

What WAS stable across all unseeded runs:
   - Root value: 1.134663 at every depth, every run
   - Entropy: 7.644 → 0.000 at every depth, every run
   - P4 monotonic: YES at every depth, every run

These three results are deterministic — they do not depend
on the random sampler and are confirmed regardless of seed.

Fix applied: np.random.seed(42) added at the top of the
__main__ block in ufuumob2_loop_fixed.py.

OBSERVATIONS FROM SEEDED OUTPUT

1. ROOT VALUE: LOCKED

   Root = 1.134663 at every depth from d=12 through d=24.
   This is a fixed point of the pure Möbius fold under these
   SL(2,C) parameters (alpha=phi, beta=1, gamma=1, delta=phi).
   It does not move with depth. This is a structural property
   of the fold, not a depth artifact.

2. P4: CONFIRMED AT EVERY DEPTH

   Entropy monotonically decreases from 7.644 bits (leaves)
   to exactly 0.000 bits (root) at all eight depths tested.
   No exceptions. This is the most robust result in the entire
   test suite and directly confirms Prediction P4 from Section
   6.1 of the submitted manuscript.

   Note: entropy at d=12 reads 7.643 rather than 7.644 due to
   bin count rounding at lower leaf counts (2^12 = 4,096 vs
   2^14+ = 16,384+). Not meaningful.

3. ULTRAMETRIC SLOPE: MOSTLY POSITIVE, NOT STABLE

   Six of eight depths show positive ultrametric exponent.
   Two depths (d=16, d=22) show negative. The sign is not
   reliably stable across depths, which means a single-depth
   measurement cannot be trusted as a definitive P1 signal.

   The positive majority is consistent with scale-invariant
   behavior predicted by conformal symmetry, but more systematic
   testing is needed before P1 can be cleanly claimed.

   Working hypothesis: the ultrametric slope may require much
   larger n_samples (currently 80,000) or a different correlation
   estimator to stabilize at this depth range. The stochastic
   sampling is introducing variance that swamps the signal at
   some depths.

   Follow-up action: increase n_samples to 500,000 or 1,000,000
   at key depths (16, 22) where the sign flips, and check whether
   the slope stabilizes positive with larger samples.

4. PEAK LCA LOCATION: FIXED ABSOLUTE DEPTH HYPOTHESIS

   The ratio column (peak LCA / d) was originally suspected to
   cluster around 0.78-0.81, suggesting the spike tracks a fixed
   fraction of total tree depth. The seeded data does not support
   this. The ratios range from 0.545 to 1.000 with no clear
   clustering.

   However, looking at the absolute Peak LCA values:
   11, 13, 13, 16, 12, 13, 12, 15

   These cluster tightly around 12-13 across most depths,
   regardless of total tree depth. This suggests a different
   hypothesis: the correlation spike may be at a roughly fixed
   ABSOLUTE LCA depth of 12-13, not a fixed fraction of d.

   If confirmed, this would represent a genuine emergent constant
   of the Möbius fold — a characteristic scale measured in
   absolute tree levels rather than relative depth. This is
   more physically interesting than a fixed ratio, as it would
   correspond to a preferred scale that does not depend on
   the total depth of the computation.

   The outliers at d=16 (peak=16, ratio=1.000) and d=12
   (peak=11) are noted. The d=16 result may indicate that at
   shallower depths the spike has not yet separated from the
   tree boundary, and the characteristic scale only becomes
   visible once d significantly exceeds 13.

   Follow-up action: run d=26, d=28, d=30 to see if peak LCA
   continues clustering around 12-13 at greater depths. If it
   does, the fixed absolute scale hypothesis is strongly supported.

SUMMARY OF CONFIRMED RESULTS (reproducible, seed-independent)

   CONFIRMED — Root convergence to 1.134663 (fixed point)
   CONFIRMED — P4: entropy monotonicity at all depths tested
   PROVISIONAL — P1: ultrametric slope mostly positive,
                 requires larger sample sizes to stabilize
   HYPOTHESIS  — Emergent characteristic scale at LCA depth
                 ~12-13, independent of total tree depth

WHAT THESE RESULTS MEAN FOR THE FOLLOW-UP PAPER

The seed fix is not just a technical correction — it is a
methodological requirement for any publication. Results from
the unseeded script are not reproducible and cannot be cited.
All figures and tables in the follow-up paper must use the
seeded version with seed value reported explicitly.

The fixed absolute LCA depth hypothesis, if confirmed at
greater depths, would be a genuinely new result not anticipated
in the submitted manuscript. It would represent the first
candidate for an emergent dimensional constant in the UFUU
framework — a preferred scale arising from the fold's own
algebraic structure rather than from any external parameter.

This would strengthen the case that the Möbius fold is not
just producing physics-like structure generally, but is
producing a specific, quantifiable preferred scale — directly
relevant to the discrete scale structure prediction (P3) in
Section 6.1.
