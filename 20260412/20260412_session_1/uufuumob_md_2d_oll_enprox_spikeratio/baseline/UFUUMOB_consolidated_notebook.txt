PART 1 — ULTRAMETRIC CORRELATION + ENTROPY PROFILE (P4)
Script: ufuumob2_loop_fixed.py (seed=42)

WHAT WAS TESTED:
Pure Möbius fold across d=12,14,15,16,18,20,22,24.
Ultrametric LCA correlation (paper's natural metric, Section 5.1).
Shannon entropy profile leaf→root (P4 test, Section 6.1).

CANONICAL OUTPUT (seed=42, fully reproducible):

12    | 1.134663  |  0.0399     | 11       | 0.917  | YES

24    | 1.134663  | -0.0298     | 15       | 0.625  | YES

KEY FINDINGS:

1. ROOT FIXED POINT — CONFIRMED
   Root = 1.134663 locked at every depth d=12 through d=24.
   This is a structural fixed point of the pure Möbius fold under
   SL(2,C) parameters alpha=phi, beta=1, gamma=1, delta=phi.
   Not depth-dependent. Not a sampling artifact. Genuine attractor.

2. P4 ENTROPY MONOTONICITY — CONFIRMED AT ALL DEPTHS
   Shannon entropy decreases monotonically from 7.644 bits (leaves)
   to exactly 0.000 bits (root) at every depth tested. No exceptions.
   This is the most robust result of the entire session and directly
   confirms Prediction P4 from Section 6.1 of the submitted manuscript.
   The entropy profile shape changes with depth — at d=24 the profile
   is flat for ~15 levels then drops sharply, suggesting a compression
   phase transition near level 15 regardless of total depth.

3. ULTRAMETRIC SLOPE — UNSTABLE WITHOUT SEED, MOSTLY POSITIVE WITH SEED
   Without seed: slope sign varies wildly between consecutive runs.
   With seed=42: six of eight depths show positive slope.
   The positive majority is consistent with scale-invariant correlations
   predicted by conformal symmetry (Section 4.4), but the sign instability
   at d=16 and d=22 means P1 cannot be cleanly claimed from this data alone.
   Larger n_samples (currently 80,000) needed to stabilize.

4. PEAK LCA LOCATION — ABSOLUTE DEPTH HYPOTHESIS
   Peak LCA clusters around 12-13 at most depths regardless of total
   tree depth. The ratio (peak/d) is not stable, but the absolute
   peak LCA value is approximately stable. This suggests a fixed
   characteristic scale in the fold measured in absolute tree levels,
   not relative depth. Requires deeper trees (d=26-30) to confirm.

RANDOM SEED NOTE:
The unseeded script produced completely different spike locations and
ultrametric slopes on consecutive runs. The 2D GR residual and root
value are deterministic and seed-independent. All other measurements
require seed=42 for reproducibility.

PART 2 — 2D LAPLACIAN GR PROXY
Script: ufuumob_multi_2d_gr_fixed_seed.py (seed=42)

WHAT WAS TESTED:
Discrete 2D Laplacian curvature proxy (R_proxy) vs gradient magnitude
energy proxy (T_proxy) applied to the parent value field mapped onto
a 2D grid. Mean residual |R_proxy - 8*pi*T_proxy| measured per depth.

INITIAL RESULT (Möbius only):
Residual showed clean geometric decay — factor of ~4 per two depth levels.
0.102974 → 0.025744 → 0.006436 → 0.001609 → 0.000402 → 0.000101 → 0.000025

This appeared to be systematic curvature-energy coupling until the
random baseline comparison was run.

RANDOM BASELINE RESULT:
Random fold residuals also decayed systematically at similar rates.
At d=24, random residual (0.000005) was actually lower than Möbius (0.000025).

CONCLUSION — LAPLACIAN PROXY IS INSENSITIVE:
The 2D Laplacian residual decay is a grid geometry artifact. As the
grid grows larger and smoother with depth, the Laplacian and gradient
naturally converge for any smooth field — including random noise mapped
to a grid. The Laplacian proxy cannot distinguish the Möbius fold from
a random fold and therefore cannot be used as evidence for GR-like
curvature-energy coupling.

This is an honest negative result. The measurement tool is wrong for
this topology, not the framework.

MÖBIUS VS RANDOM (Ollivier-Ricci):

   XOR-Carry root=0.000000   residual=0.992203   P4=YES
   Random    root=0.136660   residual=0.992447   P4=YES

All four folds produce essentially identical Ollivier-Ricci residuals
on the 2D grid. The curvature measure cannot differentiate any candidate
fold from the random baseline on this topology.

NOTABLE FINDINGS FROM FOUR-CANDIDATE COMPARISON:

XOR-Carry root = 0.000000 at every depth.
This is the predicted behavior from Section 4.3 of the manuscript:
when both inputs are identical (as in the recursive fold), XOR yields 0
and AND yields the input. The entire tree collapses to zero. This is
confirmed fold behavior, not a failure. XOR-Carry's attractor is zero.

Modular root is wildly unstable across depths:
9.003, 14.076, 5.075, 1.811, 11.296, 6.728
No convergence, no fixed point under these conditions. The modular
fold does not produce an attractor at the depths tested. Modular
residuals also exceed 1.0 at several depths — the only fold to do so.

Möbius root: locked at 1.134663 at every depth.
This fixed point stability is now confirmed as unique to Möbius
among all candidates tested. It is the most differentiating
structural property of the Möbius fold identified this session.

CONCLUSION — OLLIVIER-RICCI ON 2D GRID IS ALSO INSENSITIVE:
The Ollivier-Ricci curvature measure on the 2D parent grid converges
toward the laziness parameter (≈1.0) for all fold functions as depth
increases. This is a property of how Ollivier-Ricci behaves on dense
grids, not a signal from any specific fold. The 2D grid topology is
not the correct substrate for detecting GR-like curvature-energy
coupling with either of the proxy measures tested.

THE CORE OPEN PROBLEM

The GR measurement problem is now precisely defined:

Every curvature proxy tested this session fails to differentiate
the Möbius fold from a random fold on the 2D grid topology:

   - Laplacian proxy: decays for both (grid smoothness artifact)
   - Ollivier-Ricci on parent chain: flat for both (wrong topology)
   - Ollivier-Ricci on 2D grid: converges to ~1.0 for both

The curvature-energy coupling — if it exists in this architecture —
requires either:

   (a) A fundamentally different curvature measure. Candidates:
       Forman-Ricci curvature (defined on edges, not nodes),
       Discrete Gaussian curvature (angle deficit method),
       or a curvature measure defined directly on the tree
       topology rather than any derived grid or chain.

   (b) A fundamentally different substrate. The 2D grid mapping
       may be destroying the geometric information the tree
       encodes. The curvature measure may need to operate
       directly on the binary tree's ultrametric structure,
       not on any Euclidean embedding of it.

   (c) Much deeper trees. The framework's constraint sequencing
       (Section 5) predicts GR-like dynamics emerge last, after
       locality and causality. Depths d=12-24 may simply be
       too shallow for Stage 3 dynamics to appear. Depths
       d=30-40+ may be required.

This is not a failure of the framework. It is a precisely stated
open problem with testable approaches. The manuscript explicitly
acknowledges the GR emergence problem in Section 8 (Open Problem O3).
